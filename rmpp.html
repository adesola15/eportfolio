Literature Review

My Reflection

Writing my literature review on the financial impact of ransomware and malware in the healthcare sector was an enlightening experience. The assignment allowed me to delve deep into the nature of these cyber threats, their direct and indirect financial consequences, and the necessary cybersecurity measures. I set myself the learning outcomes of understanding the economic challenges posed by cyber-attacks in healthcare, analysing case studies, and exploring mitigation strategies. I believe I have successfully described the topic and covered these key aspects comprehensively.

At the beginning of writing, the complexity of the topic was somewhat overwhelming, but I found that starting with general research made it a bit more manageable. The more I read about the effects of cyber-attacks on healthcare providers, the more I began to understand the landscape of the topic I had chosen. As I continued researching the task became less overwhelming and it was helped by the plan, I completed in Unit 4.

The research process was intense, and I had to come up with an organisation system as this piece of work contained many more references than I am usually used to. At times, the volume of references was challenging to manage, especially as I kept rewriting sections, which caused moments of frustration. However, these challenges pushed me to develop better time management and organisational skills and ensured that I stayed on track and maintained the quality of my work.

I have gained a deeper understanding of the intersection between cybersecurity and healthcare. One significant realisation was the importance of proactive cybersecurity measures and the critical role of regulatory compliance. This new perspective has changed my approach to both my studies and my view on cybersecurity in general. Having previously studied healthcare subjects, this topic was of particular interest to me as I was able combine my interest in healthcare with my interest in computer science.

A key learning point was the identification and analysis of indirect costs associated with cyber-attacks. Before this project, I was primarily aware of direct costs like ransom payments. However, understanding the broader financial implications, such as downtime, data recovery, and long-term reputational damage, has broadened my analytical skills and deepened my appreciation for comprehensive cybersecurity strategies.

Throughout this literature review, I have maintained academic integrity by accurately citing and referencing all sources. I knew this to be important to ensure the credibility of my work and I always pay attention when citing my references. The adherence to proper citation standards has also enhanced my attention to detail and academic rigor. I did get feedback that I needed to work on critical analysis as I was just describing concepts in each paragraph with one citation to end, and I will definitely take this into consideration going forward into my Research Project Proposal.

In conclusion, this assignment fostered my academic growth by allowing me to complete a deep dive on a topic. It has increased my understanding of the financial impact of cyber-attacks on healthcare and explained to me the importance of robust cybersecurity measures, which I will take forward into my second assignment for the module.

Research Proposal Presentation

My Reflection

Completing my research proposal presentation, "A Cost-Benefit Analysis of Ransom Payment vs. Recovery Investments in Healthcare Ransomware Attacks," has been a stressful but overall satisfying experience that has deepened my understanding of both the topic and research processes. This reflection captures my journey, the challenges faced, and the lessons learned.

Going into the research proposal presentation was less daunting than the literature review because I already had a background on the topic. In fact, it was delving into the existing literature that revealed a significant gap in comprehensive analyses comparing ransom payments to recovery investments specifically within healthcare which led me to choosing this topic. I was able to understand the importance of my research topic and it motivated me to contribute meaningful insights to this area of discussion.

Even though I had a background on the topic I must admit that I did become a bit stressed when completing the parts of the research proposal presentation I had never done before. Although I had done the research for my literature review, I had to continue searching existing literature. Kruse et al.'s (2017) findings on the vulnerability of healthcare systems due to outdated IT infrastructure, alongside Everett's (2016) discussion on the unpredictability of data recovery after ransom payments, were particularly important and they hadn’t been a part of my literature review.

The learning outcomes of this module significantly influenced my approach. The papers provided a strong foundation, and I made sure to implement the feedback from my literature review and critically analyse the sources to build a coherent argument was challenging. This meant I was able to get a distinction on my research proposal presentation whereas I’d only been able to achieve a merit for my literature review. I was very happy with this.

One of the most valuable skills developed during this process was the ability to conduct a thorough research proposal presentation from start to finish. This involved not only summarising existing research but also critically evaluating methodologies, identifying gaps, and proposing directions for future research. These skills are directly applicable to real-world scenarios, such as designing effective cybersecurity policies or conducting risk assessments in healthcare organisations.

In conclusion, writing and presenting this research proposal presentation was a challenging yet rewarding experience. It honed my research skills, deepened my understanding of a critical issue, and provided valuable insights.

My Reflection on the Object-Oriented Programming Module

What?

Looking back at the Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) module, I found the workload a lot higher than previous modules. With my background not being in computer science, I was initially very concerned about designing and implementing a program from scratch. Despite these concerns, I successfully completed my final assignment gaining a deep understanding of OOP principles.

So What?

The most important part of this module was the practical application of OOP concepts. Developing the driverless car program required breaking down complex problems into manageable parts, which enhanced my critical thinking and problem-solving skills. That experience differed from previous learning because it was highly practical and hands-on, pushing me to apply theoretical knowledge in a real-world context. Also, my skills in Python improved a lot, as working with Python on real-world scenarios made me more comfortable with its syntax and features.

What Next?

From this experience, I learnt the importance of applying theoretical knowledge through hands-on practice to master complex concepts. In my projects since I haved used the OOP principles I gained to complete other pieces of work. Additionally, I adopted the focused approach I used during this module to manage tasks effectively. The OOP module was a very significant module in my MSc journey.
