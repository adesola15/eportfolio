Unit 1: Introduction to Research Methods. The Scientific Investigation and Ethics in Computing

e-Portfolio Activity: Collaborative Learning Discussion 1

Discussion Topic: Codes of Ethics and Professional Conduct

Pick a case study from the examples provided by the Association of Computing Machinery (ACM).

Review the application of the ethics code to the situation described and highlight the impact on any relevant legal (jurisdictional or non-jurisdictional) and social issues, as well as on the professionalism of the computing professionals involved. You should provide comparisons to the British Computer Society (BCS) Code of Conduct.

You should demonstrate that you understand the topic covered and ensure you use references to academic literature (including journals, books, and reports). This activity will provide evidence of your personal growth and your summary post is required in your e-portfolio.

Your initial posting should respond to the question and be at least 200 words long. Your initial post should be labelled "initial post".


Corazon has taken a highly ethical approach in their development of their heart health monitoring device and its security. This is particularly important in the healthcare sector because patient safety and data security are vital. They adhere to regulatory standards and have implemented cryptography which is line with professional ethics because it keeps information secure (Atlam and Wills, 2022). They also have charitable initiatives which shows their commitment to social welfare by improving access for disadvantaged patients.

An important aspect of Corazonâ€™s operations is their open bug bounty program, which encourages independent security evaluations. This is proactive approach towards finding vulnerabilities, is professional and shows ethical responsibility. Their response to the discovered wireless connectivity vulnerability was to collaborate with the researcher and this demonstrates transparency and a commitment to continuous improvement, which helps maintaining public trust (Von Solms & Van Niekerk, 2013).

However, the use of a hard-coded initialisation value is a problem. This design flaw currently has negligible risk, could potentially be exploited, which means they need risk assessments and s mitigation strategy (Adams & Makramalla, 2015). However, they do recognise this issue and show a willingness to address it. This reflects a strong ethical foundation, and which means that they will remain accountable for their choices (Shahri et al., 2017).

The British Computer Society (BCS) Code of Conduct emphasises the importance of public interest, duty to the profession, and continuous professional development. Corazon align well with these principles, especially in their commitment to societal well-being and their adherence to legal standards. However, the BCS Code also places a strong emphasis on the avoidance of harm, and Corazon needs to address the hard-coded value issue more to mitigate any potential future risks (British Computer Society, n.d.).

Unit 2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

Literature Review and Research Proposal Outlines

Guiding questions to support planning, revising, and refining of a literature review

1. What is the focus and aim of your review? Who is your audience?

The review will focus on the financial impact of ransomware and malware on the healthcare sector. It will explore the economic challenges bought about by cyber threats and discuss the need for strong cybersecurity measures in healthcare.

The audience will include healthcare administrators, policymakers, cybersecurity professionals, researchers in healthcare informatics, and stakeholders in the healthcare industry.

2. Why is there a need for your review? Why is it significant?

Cyber-attacks on healthcare systems are becoming more common and sophisticated, resulting in significant financial impacts and operational disruptions. Understanding the financial impact is important for healthcare professionals to implement effective cybersecurity.

The review is important because it will explore why there is an urgent need for enhanced cybersecurity measures, it will help inform policy and decision-making, and contribute to the broader discussion on healthcare cybersecurity.

3. What is the context of the topic or issue? What perspective do you take? What framework do you use to synthesise the literature?

We have to consider the increasing digitalisation of healthcare services and the corresponding rise in cyber threats targeting this sector. The review will include notable ransomware and malware attacks on healthcare providers.

The review will be looking from a financial perspective, analysing the economic impact of cyber-attacks on healthcare providers.

I will categorise the literature into sections discussing the nature of malware and ransomware, short-term and long-term financial impacts, case studies, mitigation and prevention strategies, and emerging trends.

4. How did you locate and select sources for inclusion in the review?

I will locate my sources from academic databases such as PubMed and Google Scholar, as well as through industry reports, whitepapers, and official documents from regulatory bodies. I will prioritise recent sources from the last few years.

5. How is your review structured?

It will have an introduction, then I will go into a background on ransomware in healthcare. I'll then have a section on the financial impact on healthcare providers, and case studies of financial impact in specifc attacks. Then there will be a section on mitigation and prevention strategies and then a conclusion.

6. What are the main findings in the literature on this topic?

Ransomware and malware attacks cause significant financial losses due to ransom payments, operational downtime, data recovery costs, legal fees, and increased insurance premiums.

Long-term impacts include loss of patient trust, reputational damage, regulatory fines, and higher insurance costs.

Effective mitigation strategies are regular software updates, employee training, advanced security technologies, and comprehensive incident response plans.

7. What are the main strengths and limitations of this literature?

Strengths: There are detailed analyses of financial impacts, practical case studies, and a range of mitigation strategies. It also explains why regulatory compliance and government support is important.

Limitations: There are not any standardised metrics for measuring financial impact, there are limited longitudinal studies on long-term effects, and potential bias in industry reports. Also, the quick evolving nature of cyber threats means that some information is quickly outdated.

8. Are there any discrepancies in this literature?

Different sources report varying estimates of financial losses due to differences in methodologies and scope. There are also discrepancies in the reported effectiveness of certain mitigation strategies and the role of cyber insurance in fully covering financial losses.

9. What conclusions do your draw from the review? What do you argue needs to be done as an outcome of the review?

The financial impact of ransomware and malware on the healthcare sector is significant, and it needs big investments into cybersecurity. Short-term and long-term financial impact mean there is a need for proactive strategies.

Healthcare providers should prioritise cybersecurity investments, including advanced security technologies and employee training. Governments and regulatory bodies should provide more support and resources. Further research is needed to develop standardised metrics for financial impact and to explore the long-term effects of cyber-attacks.

e-Portfolio Activity: Research Proposal Review

Considering your thoughts on your chosen area of interest for your project:

  • Which of the methods described in this week's reading would you think would suit your purpose?
  • Which data collection methods would you consider using?
  • Which required skills will you need to have or develop for the chosen project?

Note that you may find that you could be using a mixture of both the research methods and the data collection methods. These considerations will be included in your presentation of the Project Proposal in unit 10.

Using a mixed methods approach for this literature review would be best because it gives a well-rounded understanding of the financial impact of ransomware and malware on healthcare. By looking at quantitative data like financial losses, ransom payments, and recovery costs, we get clear numbers that show the economic damage these cyber-attacks cause. This data helps us see how big and frequent these financial impacts are.

On the other hand, qualitative insights from case studies, interviews with healthcare professionals, and analysis of regulatory challenges provide deeper context. These insights help us understand the disruptions to hospital operations, loss of patient trust, and how healthcare organizations respond to these attacks. They show the human and organisational sides of the financial impacts.

By combining both quantitative and qualitative data, we can get a complete picture. This approach lets us find patterns and connections in the numbers while also understanding the reasons behind them. This thorough analysis is important for suggesting effective cybersecurity strategies, making policy recommendations, and guiding future research, leading to stronger and more useful conclusions.

To use a mixed methods approach, I need to develop skills in both quantitative and qualitative analysis. This includes using software like SPSS, Excel, and NVivo. I also need to learn how to conduct and analyse interviews and surveys. Critical thinking, effective communication, and data interpretation are important too. Being able to combine different types of data and understand what they mean is essential for a thorough analysis.

Case Study: Inappropriate Use of Surveys

In 2018, Cambridge Analytica was in the news in the United Kingdom and the USA (Confessore, 2018) for obtaining and sharing data obtained from millions of Facebook users. They obtained the data through innocuous surveys on Facebook (you may have seen this type of survey and probably participated at times). This is probably the highest profile of surveys used for alternative means and, probably, monetary gains. However, this happens often through various media.

Consider how exactly this happened and why it was used. Find one or two further examples of inappropriate use of surveys and highlight the impact of all these examples from the various ethical, social, legal and professional standpoints that apply.

The Cambridge Analytica scandal in 2018 showed how personal data from online surveys can be misused. Cambridge Analytica, a political consulting firm, got data from millions of Facebook users through a personality quiz. When users took this quiz, they unknowingly gave the app access to their data and their friends' data. This massive data collection was done without proper consent and used to create detailed voter profiles to target people with political ads in the UK and the USA (Confessore, 2018).
